# Rapid educator feasibility review

## Purpose and boundary

This fixed instrument asks whether ReasonPatch's demonstrated workflow is understandable and potentially usable for formative introductory-statistics instruction. It is designed for a convenience sample of 3–5 instructors or teaching assistants who are not project contributors.

It does **not** test learning outcomes, comparative effectiveness, retention, grades, instructor workload, adoption, or product efficacy. Do not describe the results as a learner study.

Target completion time: 8–10 minutes.

## Reviewer instructions

### Privacy

Do not enter real student work, names, course identifiers, health information, or other personal data. This review does not request a reviewer name, institution, email address, or demographic information. Report responses only in aggregate or as non-identifying paraphrases.

### Reviewer confirmation

- [ ] I currently teach, assist with, or review introductory-statistics instruction.
- [ ] I am not a contributor to ReasonPatch.
- [ ] I understand that this is product feedback, not a learning study.

### Demo tasks

1. Open [the public ReasonPatch demo](https://reasonpatch.vercel.app).
2. Use the guided **Causation or coincidence?** activity.
3. Select **Find the hinge**.
4. Read the generated question and submit a short revision in your own words. Do not use real student text.
5. Create the Repair Receipt.
6. Begin the isolated fresh case and submit a short response.
7. View the Transfer Slip.
8. Open both the blinded rater packet and coordinator audit manifest.

If you cannot complete a step, stop where you are and describe the blocker below.

## Fixed review instrument

### Completion

1. Did you reach the Transfer Slip and open both educator artifacts?

   - [ ] Yes, without assistance
   - [ ] Yes, but only after retrying or receiving assistance
   - [ ] No

   If not completed, what stopped you? `Not applicable` is acceptable.

### Item ratings

For questions 2–8, select one number.

2. **Specificity of the hinge question**

   - `1` — too vague to guide a revision
   - `2` — mostly vague
   - `3` — partly specific
   - `4` — specific
   - `5` — specific enough to guide revision without supplying a complete response

3. **Amount of answer leakage in the hinge question**

   - `1` — no substantive target reasoning was supplied
   - `2` — a small hint was supplied
   - `3` — part of the target reasoning was supplied
   - `4` — most of the target reasoning was supplied
   - `5` — a near-complete replacement response was supplied

4. **Traceability of the Repair Receipt**

   - `1` — I could not determine what changed
   - `2` — changes were mostly unclear
   - `3` — changes were partly traceable
   - `4` — changes were clear
   - `5` — submitted evidence and changes were clearly traceable

5. **Separation of the fresh case from the earlier assistance**

   - `1` — earlier assistance remained clearly visible
   - `2` — substantial earlier context remained
   - `3` — some earlier context remained
   - `4` — earlier assistance was mostly removed
   - `5` — earlier diagnosis, question, rubric, receipt, and activity labels were removed from view

6. **Suitability of the blinded rater packet for human review**

   - `1` — unusable
   - `2` — requires major redesign
   - `3` — requires substantial preparation
   - `4` — usable after normal de-identification and study setup
   - `5` — ready for a small supervised review after normal de-identification

7. **Sufficiency of the coordinator audit manifest**

   - `1` — insufficient to reconstruct the workflow
   - `2` — major information is missing
   - `3` — partly sufficient
   - `4` — sufficient
   - `5` — clearly records the stage mapping, submissions, provenance, and automated evidence needed for coordination

8. **Plausibility of a real formative-assessment use**

   - `1` — I see no plausible use
   - `2` — use would require major product changes
   - `3` — a narrow use may be plausible
   - `4` — I can identify a realistic use
   - `5` — I can identify a realistic use I would consider piloting

### Recommendation and open feedback

9. Which best describes your current pilot recommendation?

   - [ ] Do not pilot in its current form
   - [ ] Consider only after major changes
   - [ ] Consider a small supervised pilot after minor changes
   - [ ] Consider a small supervised pilot as shown
   - [ ] Unsure

10. Where, if anywhere, could this workflow fit? Select all that apply.

    - [ ] Nowhere evident
    - [ ] Homework or draft revision
    - [ ] In-class formative activity
    - [ ] Office hours or tutoring
    - [ ] Assessment reflection or exam wrapper
    - [ ] Instructor review of written reasoning
    - [ ] Another setting: _______

11. What is the single biggest barrier to using or piloting this workflow? `None observed` is acceptable.

12. Which element, if any, was most useful? Optional; `None` is acceptable.

13. Did you notice a privacy, safety, grading, or misleading-claim concern? Optional; `None observed` is acceptable.

## Locked scoring guidance

Fix this guidance before collecting any responses. Do not calculate a combined “impact score.” With only 3–5 reviewers, report item-level counts, medians, and ranges.

A **positive feasibility signal** requires all of the following:

- at least three eligible, non-team reviewers complete the instrument;
- every included reviewer attempted the full workflow;
- median scores of at least `4` on questions 2, 4, 5, 6, and 7;
- median answer leakage on question 3 of at most `2`, with no score of `5`;
- at least half select one of the two small-supervised-pilot options on question 9; and
- no unresolved critical privacy, grading, or safety concern.

Classify results as:

- **Positive feasibility signal** — all conditions above are met.
- **Mixed feasibility signal** — completion occurred, but one or more thresholds were missed.
- **Revision required** — median leakage is `4+`, any core usability item has a median of `2` or below, or a credible critical safety/privacy issue is found.

Do not exclude negative responses. Report incomplete attempts and their reasons. Describe recruitment honestly as a convenience sample and disclose any prior relationship to the entrant in aggregate.
